# Midterm - Movie App

# Objectives

Apply all the User Interface knowledge and assets created so far to put together a full app design that will be coded in your other classes of the MADD program. Presenting your design is a necessary step of your midterm delivery.

Important

This midterm is a web application that resizes on different screens and not a native mobile app tied to a specific platform. When designing your app, do not limit yourself to what you can or cannot code. Aim for the best design possible for your user to enhance the usability and experience of your app. Apply all the aquired knowledge from previous classes for this assignement.

# Step 1: Plan your design:

Start by visualizing and designing your Movie app on paper before using a design software. This will always give you the clarity when you move to Figma to do things faster. Plan all the required screens, user flow, navigation and elements you need.

The design requirements for this assignment include:

  1. Navigation: First thing to think about is the type of navigation you want to use. What will be optimal for your user to access all pages clearly and always know where they are in your design. Remember to follow the usability heuristics when planning your navigation.
  2. Movies or home screen: contains welcome message, search input, and search results for the available movies. This page will contain a card component of the movie repeated with different details.
  3. Movie detail screen: with full movie details and ability to watch it. This is accessed when the user clicks on one of the cards, it contains a back button on top left to come back to the movie list page. Make sure to add a button to mark as watched when the user views the movie.
  4. Rented Page: An empty state screen for your rented page. When the user clicks first time without having any rented movies yet. Do your research on empty states and what they must contain. Once you have rented moveis the empty state will disappear.
  5. Cart screen: Any movies added to cart, and optional cart empty state for when there are no results.

When planning, consider your technical requirements but don't be limited to them. MAD9022 Requirements (opens new window).

# Step 2: Execute your Interface Design:

Use Figma to create the pages you need and the interface elements required on every page. Leverage the design system and assets previously created. Conduct comparative research of similar apps to guide your aesthetic choices and create a visually appealing accessible interface. Consider the following:

# Layout & Responsive Design:

Use your grid styles to change your interface responsively based on your device size. All 4 required screens should be resized for all three sizes:

  • Desktop
  • Tablet
  • Mobile Note: You can choose any screen size for your responsive frames, no platform specification is required at this stage.

# Design elements: Colors, Typography, components

With your design system library connected to your midterm file, leverage all the previously created styles and variables to define your pages.

# Step 3: Connect your Mobile design screens in a prototype:

For your final presentation, you are required to demo your final visual design in the form of a prototype. Use your current knowledge of Figma prototyping to connect the mobile screens only in a prototype to demonstrate the user flow. Only a mobile prototype is required to be complete, other responsive screens for desktop and tablet don't need to be prototyped and can be placed in different sections or pages.

# Step 4: Prepare your presentation:

Create a presentation in Figma Slides or your Figma design file. Make sure any new file is in your MAD9020 folder to be shared with teacher. The slides should guide your presentation's talking points and link to your demo prototype. Your presentation should be focused on your design process, your choices and how you progressed to arrive to your final visual design.

# Craft a 3-4 minute presentation:

  • Dress professionally as if you are in a real work presentation.
  • Consider your target audience and share engaging details that will retain their attention without over indulging in unnecessary details.
  • Plan your content with the time you have. In the first 2 min You need to introduce yourself, talk about your target audience, design choices of color, typography... why did you choose what you chose. In the last 2 min, focus on demoing your visual design, taking the time to walk us through the prototype and different choices you made at every step. Make sure enough time is alocated for your demo, if you take too long in the intro, you will not have enough time for demoing your prototype which is the most important part of your presentation.
  • Each student will have 1 extra minutes to receive questions or feedback. All audience students can practice giving meaningful constructive feedback or ask questions on designs presented.
  • Rehearse: Practice your presentation to ensure clarity, timing, and preparedness. Do not read from a script, practice engaging with your audience which is an essential step to a design presentation.

# Step 5: Present your work

# Format:

Delivery Method: In-class presentation during Week 07. Duration: 5 minutes per student (3-4 minutes for presentation, 1-2 minutes for Q&A).

# Midterm Evaluation Criteria

  1. Presentation Skills (15 points)
    • Clarity, volume, eye contact and engagement with the audience,
    • Good presentation slides that support your presentation content,
    • Evident Preparation and rehearsal in quality of content,
    • Effective time management,
    • Competent justification of design choices and responses to questions.
  2. Content Quality (20 points)
    • Include all required screens for responsive design,
    • Quality of design deliverable considering usability & aesthetic choices,
    • Quality of components: well thought & executed,
    • Quality of prototype and logical user flow,
    • Accessibility considerations, visibility, readability
  3. Bonus Work (5 Points) This Could be any of the following advanced features:
    • Functional Cart logic,
    • Empty states well designed,
    • Using advanced variables to create Dark/Light Mode, Theme or Language Change,
    • Splash screen,
    • Logo,
    • Additional well thought screens.
