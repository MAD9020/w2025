# Week 9: Cross-Platform Form Design

# Objective:

Design a user-friendly form for two different platforms, converting an existing form from one platform to another while adhering to each platform's design guidelines.

# Overview:

You are tasked with converting an existing form designed for iOS to an Android platform (or vice versa). This exercise will help you understand the nuances of different platform design guidelines and how to adapt a user interface to maintain usability and consistency across platforms.

# Step 1: UI Kits & Guidelines

  • Ensure to have both iOS and Material UI kits attached to your new file.
  • Access the two platform websites for additional information you may need:
  • Apple Guidelines (opens new window)
  • Material 3 Guidelines (opens new window)

# Step 2: Select an Existing Form

  • Choose a form from a real application designed for one platform (e.g., an iOS app form for user registration, feedback, or checkout process). This can be a screenshot or an existing form in the community.
  • This article has wonderful best practices for forms and some screenshots you can use to get started Form Design best practices (opens new window).
  • I recommend that you design for the platform that your are less familar with. If you are an iOS user, design for Android so you learn more about the unfamiliar platform and vice versa.

# Step 3: Analyze the Original Form to Create the exact functionality for the other platform

  • Make note of the selected form elements, layout, interactions, and visual design.
  • Review the Human Interface Guidelines (HIG) for iOS and Material Design Guidelines for Android. Links are provided above.
  • Focus on details related to forms, input fields, buttons, error handling, and overall visual design.

# Step 4: Convert the Form to the other platform

  • Re-Create the form for the alternate platform ensuring to match the functionality one to one.
  • Whatever exists in original form must be recreate in its changed shape and details for the alternate platform.
  • Ensure that you adhere to the target platform’s guidelines regarding typography, color schemes, spacing, and component usage.

# Deliverables:

  1. Converted form design in the target platform’s style.
  2. The form should include at least 8 inputs of various types, ex: address contact form...

# Grading Criteria:

  • Adherence to Guidelines(50%): How well does the converted form follow the target platform’s design guidelines?
  • Usability & quality of Visual Design(30%): Is the converted form user-friendly and intuitive? Does the converted form maintain a consistent and visually appealing design?
  • Documentation(20%): Are the design decisions clearly documented and well-explained?
