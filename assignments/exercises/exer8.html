# Week-11: Advanced Prototyping with logic

# Step 1: Learn about Figma's conditional logic for Advanced prototyping

Create advanced logic for your existing iOS app created in previous classes. Follow the tutorial below to recreate conditional logic with Figma.

# Step 2: Create your own conditional logic

As a devloper and designer, identify another problem you want to solve to make your prototype realistic. Define the logic that could lead you to a solution and use Figma logic to enable your advanced prototyping.

# Step 3: Usability Testing:

  • Download the Figma app and ensure your ptotoype works well on it.
  • Conduct a Usability testing with at least two individuals, could be colleagues or anyone.
  • Gather feedback on the usability and effectiveness of your prototype, make sure users can navigate through the transitions and logic you created.

# Step 4: Iteration

  • Based on the feedback received, iterate on your design and make improvements or corrections.

Deliverables:

  1. A high-fidelity prototype with implemented animations and micro-interactions.
  2. User testing feedback and iteration documentation.

Grading Criteria:

  1. High-Fidelity Prototype with functional logic(40%):
    • A well executed prototype with effective and purposeful animations that enhance the user experience.
  2. Aditional logic or your creation (30%):
    • Clear and functional micro-interactions that provide user feedback.
  3. User Testing and Iteration (30%):
    • User testing and documented changes of your findings.
    • Design iteration to make corrections based on user feedback.
