# Week 3 - Colour System

# Step 1: Research

Conduct some research on what kind of colours would be suitable and effective for a movie application. The Movie app will be your midterm project, and researching your colour palette is a first step towards that project.

# Step 2: Create colour palette

  • In your same desgin system file, create a new page called "Colour Palette".
  • Use any colour palette generator to select 3 main colours for your palette. It is your choice what type of colour harmony you apply as long as the 3 colours can be utilized for your Movie app.
  • Your 3 colours can be named as: Primary, Secondary, Teritiary and will be assigned later on to interface components.
  • Additionally, Create a Background colour that will be applied to your page backgrounds and surfaces. White or black recommended.
  • Create a Text colour, black or white recomended.

# Step 3: Create Colour shades

Create 11 shades for each chosen colour with number naming conventions. Your main colour should be at the center of the scale. Follow the tutorial here to create the colour shades.



Your colour system must includes shades for:

  1. Brand colours: Primary, secondary, tertiary
  2. Neutral colors for shades of grey

Other colours in your system that require no shades are:

  1. Status colours: Success, warning, error.
  2. Black and white

# Step 4: Create colour variables

Following in-class instructions or tutorial, make sure you have variables created of all your colour palette.

  • Your variables should be under a collection named Colours.
  • Creating styles may be necessary in between at this time, but is not required nor is it the goal. Variables are the main deliverable.

# Step 5: Create Modes and frames to showcase your mode changes.

In your colour variable collection Create at least 2 modes, light and dark that can later be applied to your colours.

  • the first mode is the colours you already created. That can be light.
  • Create a new mode dark. Change the colour variables for this mode.
  • Use the guidelines from this article (opens new window) for choosing your dark mode colours.
  • Use a simple component that your previously created like a simple button and show how it changes colors when moved between light and dark frames.
  • More details to acheive this task and much more with variables can be found in this tutorial.
